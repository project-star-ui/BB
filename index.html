What Are Salivary Glands?

Salivary glands are special organs in your mouth that make saliva! You have three pairs of major salivary glands:

  • Parotid glands - The biggest ones near your ears
  • Submandibular glands - Under your jaw
  • Sublingual glands - Under your tongue

Plus hundreds of tiny glands all around your mouth!

How They Work

When you see, smell, or even THINK about food, your brain tells your salivary glands to start working! They release saliva through tiny tubes called ducts.

Superpowers of Saliva

  • Makes food wet so it's easier to swallow
  • Starts breaking down food (especially carbs!)
  • Protects teeth from cavities
  • Helps you taste your food better
  • Contains special chemicals that fight germs

Fun Saliva Facts

You make about 1 liter of saliva every day!

Salivary glands work even when you're asleep!

Some animals like cows make 150 liters of saliva every day!

• Without saliva, food would taste different!

• Dentists call saliva "nature's mouthwash"!

• Spitting helps some animals (like llamas) communicate!
